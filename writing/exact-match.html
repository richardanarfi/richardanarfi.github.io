Why exact-match metrics fail for LLM systems

A short essay on alternate-valid answers, rubric-based evaluation, and why exact match often under-measures realistic LLM behavior.

Why this matters

Exact match feels objective, but it often underestimates real capability in LLM systems. Many realistic tasks allow multiple correct answers, multiple correct phrasings, or multiple correct reasoning paths.

The failure mode

When the benchmark expects one exact string, a model can be correct and still be marked wrong. That creates noisy metrics and hides meaningful improvements.

A better direction

Use exact checks where they truly fit, but add alternate-valid answers, structured assertions, and rubric-based judging for tasks that involve natural language ambiguity.

What a good benchmark does

A good benchmark separates deterministic tasks from open-ended tasks and chooses the evaluation method accordingly.
