How to evaluate tool-using agents beyond pass/fail

A short essay on why agent evaluation should track process, tool choice, recovery, and unsupported claims instead of only final success.

Pass/fail is not enough

Two agents can both fail the same task for very different reasons. One may choose the wrong tool. Another may choose the right tool, recover from an intermediate error, and still miss the final answer narrowly.

What to track instead

Track tool selection accuracy, recovery behavior, unsupported claims, and final answer quality separately. That makes the benchmark more diagnostic and more useful for iteration.

Why this helps

If the evaluation is diagnostic, prompt or model changes can be tied to specific improvements instead of treated as a black box.
